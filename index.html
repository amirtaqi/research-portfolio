About

I am a Materials Informatics Scientist at Solid Power Operating Inc., where I develop data-driven and physics-based models for next-generation energy storage devices. My work integrates multiscale simulations, machine learning, and experimental data to accelerate solid-state battery materials and cell design.

My academic training centers on multiscale computations and machine learning for energy systems and materials. At Northeastern University, my PhD research developed multiscale frameworks that couple molecular dynamics, continuum models, and experiments to design carbon- and iron-based materials for electrochemical water treatment, reactive oxidative species (ROS) generation, and CO2 sequestration.

Earlier, at the University of Illinois at Urbana–Champaign, I worked on nanofluidics and 2D materials, including strongly coupled electroosmotic transport in nanopores and deformed graphene-based biosensors for ultrasensitive detection.

I am on the academic job market, seeking a faculty position where I can build an integrated research and teaching program at the intersection of electrochemical systems, materials informatics, thermo-fluids, and climate/energy technologies.

Research

My research program connects multiscale modeling—from electronic structure and molecular dynamics to continuum-scale and system models—with machine learning and informatics to design safe, efficient, and scalable energy and environmental technologies.

Electrochemical Water Treatment & ROS Materials

I develop multiscale models of granular activated carbon (GAC) and related materials to engineer electrochemical systems that generate ROS (e.g., H2O2, ·OH) for water remediation. This includes pore-scale simulations, upscaled porous-electrode models, and experimental validation.

  • Structure–porosity–performance relationships in GAC electrodes
  • Co-design of materials, reactor geometry, and operating conditions

CO2 Sequestration & EOIF

I work on electrochemical ocean iron fertilization and alkalinity enhancement (EOIF) to couple iron delivery, alkalinity, and hydrogen recovery for large-scale CO2 removal. My work spans process design, techno-economics, and field-relevant system modeling.

  • Iron-based electrochemical cycles for mCDR and H2 co-production
  • Offshore EOIF platforms and ocean-climate systems integration

Nanofluidics & 2D Materials

I study fluids, ions, and electrons under extreme confinement, including carbon nanotube porins and 2D heterostructures. This work connects fundamental transport mechanisms to applications in desalination, energy harvesting, and biosensing.

  • Strong electroosmotic coupling in sub-nanometer pores
  • Crumpled graphene and 2D heterostructures for ultrasensitive biosensors

AI & Informatics for Batteries

At Solid Power and previously at Nissan, I build physics-informed ML frameworks that fuse cell and pack data with mechanistic models to track degradation modes, state-of-health, and performance.

  • Hybrid ML–physics models for battery prognostics
  • Data pipelines from laboratory testing to field applications

Teaching

My teaching connects core mechanical and materials engineering—thermo-fluids, heat transfer, energy storage, and materials science—with modern data and machine learning tools. Students move through a practical cycle: Concept → Model → Code → Experiment → Communication.

Teaching Philosophy

  • Backward design & mastery: clear learning outcomes in conceptual, mathematical, computational, and experimental skills, with specifications-based grading and low-stakes retrieval practice.
  • Physics-based modeling with data: students derive governing equations (e.g. Navier–Stokes, transport in porous media), build simulators from pore to device scale, and then construct physics-informed surrogates (PINNs, Gaussian processes, reduced-order models).
  • Active, inclusive classrooms: short mini-lectures alternate with think–pair–share, live coding, and whiteboard derivations. Structured teams and multiple modalities support diverse learners.
  • Ethical & reproducible AI: emphasis on units, constraints, uncertainty quantification, and transparent documentation in all ML components.
