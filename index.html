About

Bridging fundamental science and engineering innovation

Current Role

Materials Informatics Scientist at Solid Power Operating Inc., where I develop data-driven computational frameworks for next-generation solid-state batteries, applying AI and multiscale modeling to accelerate materials discovery and optimize cell performance.

Academic Background

PhD in Mechanical & Industrial Engineering from Northeastern University, with dissertation focused on multiscale computational frameworks for carbon- and iron-based materials in electrochemical water treatment and CO₂ capture. Prior research at University of Illinois at Urbana–Champaign on nanofluidics and 2D materials.

Research Vision

My research program integrates multiscale physics-based modeling, machine learning, and materials informatics to solve critical challenges in energy storage, water treatment, and climate technologies. I aim to establish a lab that connects molecular-scale phenomena to device- and system-level performance through computational innovation.

Energy Storage & Fast Charging

Developing digital twins and physics-informed ML for safe, fast-charging batteries. Integrating electro-chemo-mechanical and thermal modeling with real-time control strategies for solid-state and advanced lithium batteries.

  • Digital twins for battery management
  • Thermal-mechanical failure prediction
  • Safe fast-charging protocols
  • ML-accelerated design maps

Materials Informatics

Linking processing, microstructure, and performance in electrochemical materials through image-based ML, graph neural networks, and physics-informed surrogates for porous carbons, solid electrolytes, and composite electrodes.

  • Structure-property relationships
  • Microstructure-aware design
  • 3D imaging & tomography analysis
  • Generative models for materials

Electrochemical Water Treatment

Multiscale modeling of granular activated carbon (GAC) electrodes for reactive oxygen species generation. Connecting molecular-scale water adsorption to reactor-level performance and pollutant removal.

  • ROS generation mechanisms
  • Porous electrode design
  • Flow-through reactor optimization
  • Scale-up and MRV frameworks

CO₂ Sequestration & Climate

Electrochemical strategies coupling iron chemistry, alkalinity enhancement, and carbon capture. Developing reactor designs and MRV tools for ocean iron fertilization and carbon dioxide removal.

  • Ocean alkalinity enhancement
  • Iron-based electrochemical CDR
  • Monitoring & verification (MRV)
  • Techno-economic analysis

Nanofluidics & 2D Materials

Transport in nanoscale channels and heterostructures, including electroosmotic coupling and high-sensitivity biosensors based on deformed graphene and MoS₂ for nucleic acid detection.

  • Electroosmotic transport
  • Graphene field-effect biosensors
  • Nanoscale hydrodynamics
  • 2D heterostructure devices

Machine Learning & Informatics

Physics-informed neural networks, operator learning, uncertainty quantification, and Bayesian optimization for materials discovery, process control, and design space exploration.

  • Physics-informed ML
  • Neural operators for PDEs
  • Uncertainty quantification
  • Inverse design optimization

Future Lab Vision

The Multiscale Electrochemical Systems & Informatics Lab will develop physics-based and data-driven tools for fundamental understanding and predictive design of thermo-fluid and electrochemical systems. We will enable both forward performance prediction and inverse, target-driven design of materials, devices, and processes for energy, water, and climate applications.
